The Forms We Take

Art and Art History Student Exhibition

Organized by the Department of Visual Studies Student Society (DVSSS)

The Forms We Take reflects physical attributes, ideologies, and cultural paradigms we embody that are reflective of our personal and collective histories. Like taking away matter to find the ideal form within, our lived experiences bring us closer to a better understanding of who we are in relation to society. Our past, present, and future events shape who we are and we in turn shape them. One wonders: what are the systems, superstitions, and ideas that have become so ingrained in our everyday lives that we do not stop to think how they inform our understanding of self? Emerging contemporary artists question, probe, and confront the ways in which cultural narratives have been distorted, political histories have created divisions, and how personal experiences shape The Forms We Take.

Organized by the Department of Visual Studies Student Society, this exhibition features works by twenty-three students in all four years of the undergraduate Art and Art History program at the University of Toronto Mississauga and Sheridan College. From every year of study and varied disciplines, our artists transgress the boundaries of what is possible through their chosen media by stitching paper, assembling viewing devices, or painting without a brush to facilitate a new perspective on personal and collective histories. Each submission was carefully reviewed by a jury of student curators, resulting in a selection that highlights distinct artistic approaches grounded in theory and art historical studies.
